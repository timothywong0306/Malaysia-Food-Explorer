Homemade Nasi Lemak (T1)

⏰ Prep: 15 mins 🍳 Cook: 30 mins 📊 Difficulty: Easy

⏱️ Total Time: 45 mins

🍽️ Servings: 2 people

🌶️ Spice Level: Mild

🧂 Ingredients (2 servings)

  • 1 cup fragrant rice
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 pandan leaves (knotted)
  • 1 slice ginger
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • Sides:
  • 50g fried peanuts
  • 50g fried anchovies
  • 1 cucumber (sliced)
  • 2 boiled eggs
  • 4 tbsp sambal

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice and drain well.
  2. Add rice, coconut milk, water, pandan leaves, ginger, and salt to rice cooker. Mix well.
  3. Cook as usual.
  4. Let sit for 10 minutes, then remove pandan and ginger.
  5. Fry peanuts and anchovies until golden and crispy.
  6. Slice cucumber and boil eggs, cut in half.
  7. Plate the rice and arrange sides around it.
  8. Serve with sambal.

💡 Pro Tip: If you don't have pandan leaves, use a drop of pandan essence. Sambal can be store-bought or homemade.

🌟 Did you know? Nasi Lemak was originally a farmer's breakfast to provide energy for long working days!

Easy Chicken Satay(T2)

⏰ Prep: 30 mins (incl. marination) 🍳 Cook: 15 mins 📊 Difficulty: Medium

⏱️ Total Time: ~45 mins

🍽️ Servings: 4 people

🌶️ Spice Level: Medium

🥩 Ingredients (20 skewers)

  • 500g chicken thigh (cubed)
  • 2 tbsp curry powder
  • 1 tbsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Bamboo skewers (soaked 30 mins)
  • Peanut Sauce:
  • 200g roasted peanuts
  • 3 tbsp palm sugar
  • 1 tbsp tamarind juice
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • Sides: Cucumber slices, red onion slices

🔥 Instructions

  1. Marinate chicken with spices, sugar, salt, and oil for at least 2 hours (overnight preferred).
  2. Skewer 3–4 pieces per stick.
  3. Blend peanut sauce ingredients until smooth.
  4. Simmer sauce over low heat until thick, about 5–7 mins.
  5. Grill or pan-fry skewers over medium heat, turning occasionally, 8–10 mins.
  6. Serve with peanut sauce, cucumber, and onion.

💡 Pro Tip: You can substitute beef or lamb. If no grill is available, bake at 200°C for 10–12 minutes.

🌟 Did you know? Satay originated in Java and spread across Southeast Asia. In Malaysia, it's a staple at night markets and family gatherings.
